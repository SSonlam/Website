What is Data Provenance?

“Provenance” refers to the process of creating, evolving, and maintaining the data. Provenance answers the questions of who created\worked with the data, when\where it was created, and how it was changed until the present place.

Where is it used?

  • In healthcare, Provenance is used to store patients medical history including demographics, progress notes, problems, medications, vital signs, past medical history, immunizations, laboratory data and radiology reports. [1]
  • In art and antiques, provenance is used to prove the authenticity and value of an artifact mainly through ownership history.
  • In the software industry, developers rarely write code from scratch; they take advantage of the available resources like libraries and code from third parties. Therefore, data provenance is significant to determine how trusted the data (function, feature, resources, code, etc.) is and the permissions as well as licenses that it requires. [2]
